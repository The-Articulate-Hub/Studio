Introduction to Online Safety

Welcome to the Online Safety eBook. This guide will walk you through essential information to help you stay safe online.

  • Learn to create strong passwords.
  • Recognise scams and phishing attempts.
  • Avoid cyberbullying and stay secure.
  • Access helpful resources for additional support.

Creating Strong Passwords

Passwords are your first line of defence. Follow these steps for strong passwords:

  • Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Avoid using common words like "password" or "123456".
  • Make your passwords at least 12 characters long.
  • Use a password manager to store complex passwords.

Recognising Scams

Scammers use different tactics to trick you. Here’s what to look for:

  • Emails or messages asking for personal details.
  • Links that look legitimate but redirect you to a fake site.
  • Urgent messages that try to scare you.

Avoiding Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying can have a serious impact on mental health. Here’s how to protect yourself:

  • Block and report users who send hurtful messages.
  • Never engage with negative comments online.
  • Talk to someone you trust if you feel upset.

Support Resources

If you ever feel unsafe online, here are some resources to help you:

  • National Online Safety Hub – Provides guides for online security.
  • Report Harmful Content – Helps you report abuse or scams.
  • Childline – Support for young people facing online harassment.
