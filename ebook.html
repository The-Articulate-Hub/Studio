Introduction to Online Safety

Introduction

Welcome to the Online Safety eBook. Learn how to navigate the internet safely, recognise scams, and protect your personal data.

Social Media Safety

Follow these tips to stay safe on social platforms:

  • Adjust privacy settings to limit who can see your posts.
  • Avoid sharing your location publicly.
  • Think before clicking any unknown links.

Identifying Scams

Scammers often use urgency to trick people. Be cautious:

  • Watch out for emails asking for financial details.
  • Don’t trust pop-ups claiming you’ve won a prize.
  • Ignore messages from unknown senders promising quick rewards.

Protecting Personal Information

Your data is valuable. Here’s how to protect it:

  • Use unique passwords for different accounts.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • Review permissions for apps that access your data.

Responding to Cyberbullying

If you’re facing cyberbullying, here’s what you can do:

  • Block and report users who send offensive messages.
  • Save screenshots as evidence.
  • Talk to someone you trust or seek support.

Support Resources

If you need help, reach out to these resources:

  • Childline: Support for young people facing online challenges.
  • Report Harmful Content: Report offensive or illegal online content.
  • CyberSafe: Guides to improve online safety.
